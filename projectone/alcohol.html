AlCOHOL, GAMBLING, AND ADDICTION

As a person of christian faith, I think that ‘I’ is not just for ‘me’ and therefore I do not think that ‘I’ have the authority to destroy my being. I believe that I am a product of my parents who gave me the gift of life, my grandparents, great grandparents and beyond who fought for their lives through the Korean war, Japanese colonialism, and countless other hardships throughout history. That is ancestry and history. Therefore I must believe that I am here because of such history and that there is a reason I am here. That is why I feel a sense of responsibility to preserve healthy genes to give birth to my children and grandchildren, as well as nurture and raise them with love. To do so, I believe that one must be able to have control over addictions that can destroy my sense of being. An example would be alcohol- Alcohol has the ability to occasionally help you connect with people and improve your mood. But that should ultimately be the extent of the role it plays in your life. One should be able to control substances such as alcohol rather than let it control my life. The same could be said about gambling or drugs.
